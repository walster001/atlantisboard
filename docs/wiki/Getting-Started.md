# Getting Started

Welcome to AtlantisBoard! This guide will help you create an account, set up your first workspace and board, and get familiar with the interface.

## Creating an Account

AtlantisBoard supports multiple authentication methods:

### Local Account

1. Navigate to the login page
2. Click "Sign Up" or "Create Account"
3. Enter your email address and create a password
4. Optionally provide your full name
5. Complete the registration process

### Google OAuth

1. Navigate to the login page
2. Click the "Sign in with Google" button
3. Authenticate with your Google account
4. Grant permissions to AtlantisBoard
5. You'll be automatically logged in and your account will be created

### Email Verification

Depending on your organization's settings, you may need to verify your email address after registration. Check your inbox for a verification email and follow the instructions.

## First Login

After creating your account and logging in, you'll see the Home page. This is your central hub for accessing workspaces and boards.

### Home Page Overview

The Home page displays:

- **Workspaces**: List of workspaces you belong to
- **Boards**: List of boards you have access to (organized by workspace)
- **Navigation**: Access to settings, admin panel (if applicable), and your profile
- **Quick Actions**: Buttons to create new workspaces and boards

## Creating Your First Workspace

Workspaces help organize related boards and teams. To create a workspace:

1. Click the "New Workspace" button on the Home page
2. Enter a workspace name
3. Optionally add a description
4. Click "Create"

You automatically become the owner of the workspace you create. As the owner, you can:
- Create boards in the workspace
- Add members to the workspace
- Manage workspace settings

## Creating Your First Board

Boards are where your work lives. To create your first board:

1. Select a workspace from your workspace list
2. Click "New Board" within that workspace
3. Enter a board name
4. Optionally add a description
5. Choose an initial background color
6. Click "Create"

Your new board will open automatically. You'll see an empty board with options to add columns.

## Adding Columns

Columns represent stages in your workflow (e.g., "To Do", "In Progress", "Done"). To add columns:

1. Click "Add Column" at the right side of the board
2. Enter a column name
3. Press Enter or click outside the input field

You can add as many columns as needed. Columns can be reordered by dragging them horizontally.

## Creating Your First Card

Cards represent individual tasks or items. To create a card:

1. Click "Add Card" at the bottom of any column
2. Enter a card title
3. Press Enter to create the card

Click on a card to open its detail view, where you can add descriptions, attachments, labels, due dates, and more.

## Navigation Overview

### Main Navigation

- **Home Icon**: Return to the home page with all workspaces and boards
- **Board Name**: Shows the current board name (click to return to home)
- **Settings**: Access board settings (if you have permission)
- **User Menu**: Access your profile, settings, and sign out

### Board View Navigation

- **Column Headers**: Click to rename, delete, or customize columns
- **Card Menu**: Three-dot menu on each card for quick actions
- **Board Settings**: Gear icon in the top-right for board configuration
- **Invite Button**: Add members to the board

### Keyboard Shortcuts

- **Space + Drag**: Pan the board view
- **Click Card**: Open card detail modal
- **Drag Cards**: Move cards between columns
- **Drag Columns**: Reorder columns

## User Profile Management

Access your profile from the user menu in the top-right corner. Your profile includes:

- **Email Address**: Your account email (cannot be changed)
- **Full Name**: Your display name (editable)
- **Avatar**: Profile picture (if configured)
- **Account Settings**: Password change, preferences, etc.

1. **Start Simple**: Begin with a basic board structure and add complexity as needed
2. **Use Workspaces**: Organize related projects into workspaces for better management
3. **Invite Collaborators**: Add team members early to establish good collaboration patterns
4. **Explore Settings**: Board settings offer many customization options
5. **Try Themes**: Board themes can make your boards more visually organized
