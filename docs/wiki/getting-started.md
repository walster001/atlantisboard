# What this app is

[← Wiki home](Home.md)

Atlantisboard is a **self-hosted** place to plan work visually. You organize work on **boards**: each board has **lists** (columns) and **lists** hold **cards**. A card is usually one task or one idea.

**Workspaces** group boards together — think “Home projects” vs “Work team” without mixing tiles on one giant pile.

**Real-time** means when someone else moves a card or edits something you are allowed to see, your screen can update without you refreshing the page.

---

## Who it is for

- Teams that want a simple Kanban-style board (To do → Doing → Done, or any columns you like).  
- People who host their own software and do not want their task data on someone else’s cloud.  
- Anyone who has used similar tools before — the ideas map closely, so you will feel at home quickly.

---

## What you need

- A **browser** on a computer or phone (recent versions work best).  
- An **account** on an instance someone already set up, **or** you are the person doing the install (see [Linux production install](linux-production-install.md)).

---

## Words you will see

| Word | Plain meaning |
|------|----------------|
| Board | One canvas of lists and cards. |
| List / column | A vertical stack of cards; often a stage of work. |
| Card | One item; open it for description, checklists, files, comments, etc. |
| Workspace | A folder-like group of boards on your home screen. |
| Invite | A link or flow that adds someone to a board or workspace with a chosen role. |
